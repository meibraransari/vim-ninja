LESSON 1: VIM MODES
===================

Vim has multiple modes. Understanding them is the key to Vim mastery.

NORMAL MODE (default when you open a file)
------------------------------------------
- You are currently reading this in NORMAL mode
- Keys are COMMANDS, not text input
- Navigate with: h j k l
- Press 'i' to enter INSERT mode

INSERT MODE
-----------
- Press 'i' to enter (cursor before current char)
- Press 'a' to enter (cursor after current char)
- Press 'I' to insert at beginning of line
- Press 'A' to insert at end of line
- Press 'o' to open new line below
- Press 'O' to open new line above
- Press Esc to return to NORMAL mode

VISUAL MODE
-----------
- Press 'v' for character selection
- Press 'V' for line selection
- Press Ctrl+v for block selection
- Press Esc to exit

TIPS
====
- The mode is shown at the bottom of the screen
- -- INSERT -- means you are in Insert mode
- No indicator = Normal mode
- -- VISUAL -- means you are in Visual mode
